Giving Back

I was lucky

Early in my life I understood something that not everyone gets to see in time: education is the only true social elevator. It does not care where you come from. It opens doors that nothing else can.

I was lucky enough to grasp this early, and to have people around me who confirmed it. But luck is not a system — and the Italian situation makes this painfully clear.

"The best investment you can make is in yourself." — Warren Buffett

Italy has one of the lowest rates of university graduation in Europe, and social mobility has been stalling for decades. Too many students — especially in the South — never encounter a convincing reason to believe that studying changes lives. Nobody around them has shown them that it can.

I try, in small ways, to be that example for someone else.

Share of 25–34 year-olds with a tertiary degree, EU countries, 2024

Source: Eurostat (edat_lfse_03; educ_uoe_grad04). STEM = science, mathematics, computing, engineering, manufacturing & construction.

The picture is stark. In 2024, only 31.6% of Italians aged 25–34 held a university degree — second-to-last in the EU, ahead only of Romania. The EU average stands at 44.2%. On STEM specifically, Italy produces around 19 graduates per 1,000 young people, while countries like Finland and Ireland produce more than 28. The gap is not narrowing fast enough.

Initiative

Christmas in STEM

Occasionally, I return to my former high school during the Christmas period to talk with students about how STEM subjects shape the world around us — from the algorithms that recommend their music to the models that price the risk behind their parents' mortgage. The goal is not recruitment. It is simply to make the invisible visible: to show that mathematics, statistics, and programming are not abstract exercises but genuine instruments of understanding and power.

I believe these conversations matter most when they happen early, before students have already closed doors in their own minds. If you are a teacher or school coordinator in Italy and would like to organise something similar, feel free to reach out.
